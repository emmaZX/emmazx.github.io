career goals

Where I'm Going

the long view

I want to work at the intersection of security and systems engineering, building software that is not just functional but defensible. Whether that means security engineering at a company whose product is security, applied research in binary analysis, or building tools that help defenders understand how attackers think, I want the work to matter beyond just shipping features.

I'm a sophomore with enough runway to be deliberate about it. The plan below is my current thinking and it will evolve, but it's grounded in the skills and experiences I know I need.

Now – Summer 2026
Land a Technical Internship

Targeting security engineering, embedded systems, or backend SWE roles at companies where the work is technical and the codebase matters.

  • Competing in CTFs through Greyhat, sharpening Python and systems fundamentals
  • Continuing malware analysis work and deepening binary exploitation skills
  • Applying to 50+ roles early, not late
Junior Year – 2026/2027
Go Deeper on Security and Systems

With an internship behind me, I want coursework and research that pushes technical depth in the areas I care about most.

  • Advanced security and systems courses at GT: network security, OS internals
  • Move from practicing CTFs to actually competing in them
  • Undergraduate research position in a security or systems lab at GT
Senior Year – 2027/2028
Capstone and Career Decision

Senior year is about synthesis. Pulling together coursework, projects, and internship experience into a clear professional identity.

  • Substantial senior capstone in a security or systems domain
  • Decide between industry and a master's or PhD in security
  • Target roles at security companies or product companies with strong security engineering teams
5 or more years out
Security Engineering or Research

I want to be the kind of engineer who deeply understands how systems fail and can design ones that don't. Hard problems, high stakes, work that actually matters.
