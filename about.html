introduction

I'm a sophomore studying Computer Engineering at Georgia Tech (GPA 3.71), originally from Decatur, Georgia. I have a deep interest in understanding how things work at a fundamental level, which has led me to spend a significant amount of time in IDA Pro tracing the inner workings of real-world malware with no source code, no documentation, and a lot of patience.

This site is my professional portfolio, a place to document what I've built, where I'm headed, and what I'm curious about. Whether you're a recruiter, a collaborator, or just exploring, welcome.

what I work on

My most substantive technical work has been in binary analysis and malware reverse engineering. Over the past year I've worked through six real-world malware samples of increasing complexity, annotating x86 assembly line by line to reconstruct exploit logic, control flow, shellcode construction, and evasion techniques. The most challenging was an encrypted, polymorphic Windows PE infector that mutated its own code to resist analysis and spread silently through removable drives and peer-to-peer networks.

Beyond security, I've built software at multiple levels of abstraction: a full-stack web application with an AI recipe pipeline and AWS authentication built at a hackathon, a real-time game running on ARM Mbed in C/C++ with no operating system, a competition robot with a custom PCB, and a Unity puzzle game built with VGDev.
